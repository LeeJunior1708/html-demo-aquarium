Coral

coral - under-sea forest

Under the clear blue water of many tropical waters there are formed forests that are not from trees from special animals - it is coral. Coral reefs are considered the most diverse, rich and wonderful ecology in the ocean.

What is coral?

Although it looks like a tree branch, or a stone, the coral is actually a group of animals that are similar to jellyfish and anemone. They belong to the group of marine animals with itching brooches (called robotics).

How does coral grow?

Corals can grow in two ways: lay eggs (faithful reproduction) and shoot (asexual reproduction).

Sincere reproduction:
The coral polyps, male eggs and sperm into water and fertilization In addition to creating very small coral larvae, after a while of floating sediments to the bottom suitable to develop into PoLyP New corals.

Asexual reproduction:
Corals can grow buds. Small polyps appear on the side of the old coral polyps and gradually gradually become separate PoLyPs with the skeleton due to them for themselves.

Distribution of coral reefs:

Coral reefs exist only in the warm and shallow waters (depth of about 40m back) and in. That is why coral reefs in the world only distribute limits in the area with an average temperature above 20oC. The Pacific Ocean is the largest ocean on the planet and has the most diverse and abundant coral reefs.

Effect of the environment

Coral is sensitive to changes in the natural environment. Scientists have predicted that by 2030 more than 50% of the world coral reefs may be destroyed; Therefore, they are often protected by environmental laws. A coral reef can easily be flooded in algae if there are too many nutrition in the country. Corals will also die if the water temperature changes exceeds 1-2 degrees out of normal

Coral reef

Coral species depend on single-celled gold algae and stretches (stone corals) are often found at coral reefs - large structures with calcium carbonate in shallow tropical waters. Coral reefs are created from coral skeletons and are tied together by Calcium carbonate layers due to Coralline algae (Corallinaceae). Coral reefs are extremely diverse marine ecosystems, which are home to more than 4,000 species of fish, countless species like Ti (Cnidaria), soft body, crustaceans and many other animals
