Anemone

anemone - The terrifying carnivores of the sea

With a colorful and extremely harmless appearance, the kneeling is an extremely scary predators of the sea. They often use their sexy weapons to be the tentacles to 'flirt' and suddenly inject malmples into prey. When prey is paralyzed, they will 're-' prey without hesitate ...

What is anemone?

The English name is Sea Anemone. They are a group of predators in the Actiniaria department. Because there is a half-animal structure, half a plant, so it is named after the anemone - a colorful flowering plant on the ground. Sea habits are classified as CNIDARIA, Anthozoa class and Hexacorallia classification. Anthozoa class contains polyps that help the anemone can digest the prey larger than it is several times. The anemone has close relations with corals, jellyfish, Hydra and Ceriantharia.

The creation of the anemone:

The anemone has an interesting structure. It has a pipe shape with a diameter of about 1-5cm, length about 1.5-10cm. Due to the 'inflatable' mechanism, the anemone can be changed in size. Some alone species are quite large as Urticina Columbiana and Stichodactyla Mertensii are up to 1m.

Around the mouth of the anemone is the shaped tentacles with cnidocytes spikes. These cells have just been defensive, medium to attack the prey. Why can attack and defend? There is this because in Cnidocytes cells contain glands containing venom. Just a touch, cells in the enemy's body or prey will be broken from there made the enemy and prey paralyzed.
