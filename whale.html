Whale

Whales - The biggest animal in the ocean

As you know Whale is a large-sized mammal that lives in the sea. Whales are from the same lineage as dolphins and sperm whales. With such a large size, how do whales move, how are their characteristics different from dolphins and sperm whales?

What is Whale?

Whales have the English name Whale and their scientific name is Cetacea. Currently on earth, people have found about 90 different species of whales.
Most of them live in the great oceans, with only about 5 species living in freshwater environments.

Origin of Whale

Whale is an evolution of terrestrial mammals. Their ancestors were ungulate carnivores of the genus chevrotain.
This species became extinct about 48 million years ago, the genus Cetaceans and the genus Archaeocetes
After geological changes, animals belonging to the genus Cetaceans and the genus Archaeocetes, gradually evolved and moved down to live in the aquatic environment.
About 5-10 million years ago, they really evolved and were fully adapted to life in the aquatic environment.
The line of whales is divided into 2 main species: baleen whales and toothed whales

How do whales locate sound?

Whale has the ability to locate sounds extremely sensitive. From the sounds they emit, they can determine the position - the distance.
The size of their prey and the aquatic environment they are in so that they can easily adapt and hunt for prey.
The ability to locate sound also supports their eyes when living in a low-light water environment.
In addition, the ability to locate sounds dolphins also use to communicate with their herd.
Because of this special ability, many whales have been raised and trained to do circus.
Whale has a relatively long life span, ranging from 30 to 70 years old. Blue whales have an extremely long average lifespan, they can live from 80 to 110 years old.
When whales in the wild die, their bodies will swell and possibly explode.

Eatability

Based on the way whales eat, we can divide them into 2 types:
Toothed Whale (Odontoceti): This lineage includes sperm whales, white whales and species of dolphins.
Species with jaws have implants that create many teeth, so they can eat large fish, squid and other marine animals.
In which, orca species is a species that specializes in catching seagulls as their food.
The baleen whale (Mysticeti): this line is characterized by no teeth such as blue whales, humpback whales, gray back whales, bowhead whales...
The food of these species is small animals such as fish (herring, sardines, sea bass), small animals on the sea floor (shrimp, crab) and molluscs (plankton).
According to statistics, an average whale of an average size can consume an amount of food up to 227kg per day.

Personality characteristics of Whale

Whales are a large strain of fish but they do not harm people or ships in the sea. They can distinguish which prey they attack and which humans should not attack.
Therefore, whales are raised and trained a lot by humans.

The brain structure of whales has spindle neurons that closely resemble those of humans.
Therefore, all emotions, actions and their judgment are very high and accurate.
Whale when living in captivity and training them to be able to perform and follow human instructions.
Some whales, when raised by humans, can even imitate voices very well.
Not only smart, quick reflexes, whales are also extremely sensitive and emotional. They can sense human emotions to share.
